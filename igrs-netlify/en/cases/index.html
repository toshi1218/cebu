USCIS · K-1

K-1 Fiancé Visa

The K-1 fiancé visa allows a US citizen to bring their Philippine fiancé(e) to the United States for marriage. USCIS requires extensive Philippine civil documentation as part of the I-129F petition and the DS-160 consular interview process.

Required Philippine Documents

  • PSA Birth Certificate (with DFA apostille)
  • CENOMAR — Certificate of No Marriage Record (PSA)
  • NBI Clearance (with DFA apostille)
  • PSA Marriage Certificate from any prior marriages (if applicable)
  • PSA Death Certificate of former spouse (if widowed)

USCIS · CR-1 / IR-1

CR-1 / IR-1 Spouse Visa

The CR-1 (Conditional Resident) and IR-1 (Immediate Relative) spouse visas allow US citizens and lawful permanent residents to petition for their Philippine spouse's immigration. These cases require a full Philippine civil document package submitted with Form I-130.

Required Philippine Documents

  • PSA Birth Certificate of the Philippine spouse
  • PSA Marriage Certificate (with DFA apostille)
  • NBI Clearance (with DFA apostille)
  • CENOMAR (if required by NVC or consulate)
  • PSA documents for any children included in the petition

F-1 · J-1 · Student Visas

Student & Exchange Visas

F-1 student visa and J-1 exchange visitor program applications for Philippine nationals often require civil documentation and background clearances. Requirements vary by university, exchange program sponsor, and consular post — we provide the full range of procurable Philippine documents.

Required Philippine Documents

  • PSA Birth Certificate
  • NBI Clearance (required by many consulates and programs)
  • CENOMAR or other PSA civil status documents (if required)
  • DFA apostille authentication (when required by receiving institution)

H-1B · L-1 · Work Permits

Work Visas & Employment

Employment-based immigration cases for Philippine nationals — including H-1B specialty occupation visas, L-1 intracompany transfers, Canadian work permits, and Australian skilled worker visas — frequently require Philippine background clearances and civil documents.

Required Philippine Documents

  • NBI Clearance (standard requirement for most work visa categories)
  • PSA Birth Certificate
  • LTO Driver's Record (for roles requiring driving history)
  • DFA apostille on all Philippine-issued documents
  • Certified translations where required

Naturalization · Citizenship

Naturalization & Citizenship

Naturalization applications in the US (N-400), Canada, and Australia require applicants to submit comprehensive documentation of their civil and criminal history in any country of prior residence or citizenship — including the Philippines. These cases often need the most complete Philippine document packages.

Required Philippine Documents

  • PSA Birth Certificate (with DFA apostille)
  • NBI Clearance (with DFA apostille)
  • PSA Marriage Certificate (if applicable)
  • CENOMAR (proof of single status, if applicable)
  • PSA documents for all family members listed in the application

Country-Specific Requirements

We are familiar with the Philippine document requirements for immigration authorities in each country we serve.

United States (USCIS / NVC / DOS)

USCIS, the National Visa Center, and US consular officers follow the Foreign Affairs Manual (FAM) for Philippine document requirements. We supply documents that meet these standards — original PSA security paper, apostilled NBI clearances — for all immigrant and nonimmigrant visa categories.

Canada (IRCC)

Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada requires original Philippine civil documents for spousal sponsorships, work permits, and permanent residency applications. DFA apostille is accepted for Philippine documents submitted to IRCC. We handle the full procurement and authentication chain.

Australia (Home Affairs)

The Australian Department of Home Affairs requires Philippine civil documents for partner visas (subclass 309/100, 820/801), skilled worker visas, and citizenship applications. We supply apostilled PSA and NBI documents meeting Australian requirements.

Other Jurisdictions

IGRS documents are procured with DFA apostille, making them legally recognized in all Hague Convention member states. If your client is applying in a jurisdiction not listed here, contact us to discuss specific requirements.
