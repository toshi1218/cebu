PSA

PSA Certificates

Philippine Statistics Authority (PSA) certificates are the foundational documents required in nearly every Philippine immigration case. All PSA documents are issued directly by the Philippine government and carry official security paper and seals.

What's Included

  • PSA Birth Certificate (original security paper)
  • PSA Marriage Certificate
  • CENOMAR — Certificate of No Marriage Record
  • PSA Death Certificate (if applicable)
  • DFA apostille authentication (add-on)
  • DHL international shipping to your office

Required For

  • K-1 Fiancé Visa petitions (USCIS)
  • CR-1 / IR-1 Spouse Visa applications
  • F-1 and J-1 student visa applications
  • Naturalization and citizenship cases
  • Spousal sponsorship (Canada PR, Australia PMV)
  • Any immigration case requiring proof of civil status

NBI

NBI Clearance

NBI (National Bureau of Investigation) Clearance is the Philippine equivalent of a criminal background check. It is required for nearly all immigration applications involving Philippine nationals, including work visas, spouse visas, and naturalization.

What's Included

  • NBI Clearance (official government-issued)
  • In-person representation at NBI offices in Cebu
  • Coordination with applicant for biometric requirements
  • DFA apostille authentication (add-on)
  • DHL international shipping to your office
  • Weekly status updates throughout procurement

Required For

  • K-1 Fiancé Visa petitions
  • H-1B, L-1, and employment-based visas
  • F-1 student visa applications (some consulates)
  • Canadian work permits and PR applications
  • Australian partner and skilled worker visas
  • Naturalization and citizenship applications

LTO

LTO Driver's Records

Land Transportation Office (LTO) records are needed when a Philippine national wishes to convert their Philippine driver's license in their destination country, or when driving history is required as part of an immigration or background verification process.

What's Included

  • Official LTO Driver's Record (driving history)
  • License verification and status confirmation
  • Direct coordination with LTO regional offices
  • DFA apostille authentication (add-on)
  • DHL international shipping

Required For

  • Foreign license conversion in US, Canada, Australia
  • Employment-based immigration cases requiring driving history
  • Background verification for professional visa applications
  • Insurance and employment background checks

DFA

DFA Apostille

DFA (Department of Foreign Affairs) apostille authentication is required to make Philippine government documents legally recognized in countries party to the Hague Apostille Convention — including the United States, Canada, and Australia. Without apostille, Philippine documents may be rejected by immigration authorities.

What's Included

  • DFA apostille stamp on PSA, NBI, and other government documents
  • Authentication of translated documents
  • Coordination between DFA offices and document issuers
  • Quality check before shipping
  • DHL international shipping with tracking

Why It Matters

  • Required by USCIS for foreign document submissions
  • Mandatory for Canadian immigration (IRCC) submissions
  • Required for Australian Department of Home Affairs
  • Ensures documents are accepted without additional verification
  • Prevents delays and rejections due to unauthenticated documents
DFA Apostille (standalone) Included in all packages

All IGRS document packages include DFA apostille authentication. We handle the full chain: document procurement → apostille → international delivery.

Translation

Translation

Philippine government documents are issued in Filipino or English. While English documents are typically accepted directly, some immigration filings require certified translations or explanatory notes for non-English portions. IGRS provides translation services as part of our full-service offering.

What's Included

  • Certified translation of PSA certificates
  • Translation of NBI Clearance annotations
  • Notarized translations where required
  • Translation review for accuracy and completeness
  • Delivered together with original documents

When You Need It

  • Documents with Filipino-language annotations
  • Cases requiring explanatory translation for adjudicators
  • Canadian immigration filings requiring French or certified English
  • Any case where your client's documents contain non-English text
